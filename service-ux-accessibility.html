Why Accessibility Matters

The Business Case for Inclusive Design.

Legal Protection

The Equality Act 2010 requires businesses to make reasonable adjustments for people with disabilities. Public sector sites and increasingly private sector sites face legal challenge for non-compliance.

Broader Audience Reach

Approximately 1 in 5 people in the UK live with a disability. An inaccessible website is actively excluding a significant portion of your potential audience — and their purchasing power.

SEO Advantage

Accessibility and SEO are deeply aligned. Proper heading structure, descriptive alt text, semantic HTML, and fast load times all contribute directly to improved search engine rankings.

Higher Conversion Rates

Accessible sites are easier to use for everyone — not just those with disabilities. Cleaner navigation, better contrast, and logical content structure improve conversion rates across all user segments.

Standards & Compliance

What WCAG 2.1 AA Actually Means.

WCAG 2.1 is structured around four core principles. I evaluate and address every criterion within each principle.

P

Perceivable

All content must be presentable in ways users can perceive — including alt text for images, captions for video, sufficient colour contrast, and text that can be resized without loss of functionality.

O

Operable

All functionality must be operable via keyboard alone. No time limits that cannot be extended. No content that causes seizures. Clear navigation with consistent, predictable patterns.

U

Understandable

Text content must be readable and understandable. Pages must operate in predictable ways. Users must be helped to avoid and correct mistakes — particularly in forms.

R

Robust

Content must be robust enough to be interpreted reliably by a wide range of assistive technologies — including screen readers, voice control software, and switch navigation devices.

My Process

Audit. Remediate. Test. Certify.

A structured approach that gives your organisation clear visibility of where you stand and a defined path to full compliance.

01

Automated & Manual Audit

Automated scanning tools identify the most common issues instantly. This is followed by thorough manual testing — keyboard navigation, screen reader testing, and cognitive accessibility review — which catches what automated tools miss.

02

Prioritised Findings Report

A clear report of all findings — ranked by severity and categorised by WCAG criterion. Each issue includes its location, impact, and a specific recommended fix. No jargon; actionable from day one.

03

UX Review & Journey Mapping

Alongside the technical audit, a review of key user journeys from an inclusive design perspective — identifying barriers that may not constitute a WCAG failure but still create poor experiences for users with additional needs.

04

Remediation

Hands-on implementation of fixes — either directly or in close collaboration with your development team. All changes validated in a staging environment before deployment.

05

Re-Audit & Verification

Post-remediation testing to verify all issues are resolved and no new issues have been introduced. A clean re-audit report confirming WCAG 2.1 AA compliance.

06

Accessibility Statement & Ongoing Support

Publication of a legally compliant accessibility statement. Guidance for your team on maintaining compliance as the site evolves, with ongoing review available as a retained service.
